Hi, my name is Chima. This is what you need to focus on in 2026 without all of the confusion.

Number one, build entity clusters that cover the full query fan out. Links are still important, but we are evolving towards language based models. The relationships are more about entities and semantic relationships than link based relationships. We need to start optimizing more for entities than anything else. Think topical clusters than keyword clusters. You pick an entity, you map intents to the cluster for each entity — awareness, comparison, evaluation and decision.

Number two, evolve reporting beyond traffic. Still track LLM referrals. LLMs are more of an influence channel than a traffic channel. Also add self-attribution reporting. Track branded demand as an influence proxy — branded impressions, branded search lifts, direct traffic trends. Track LLM citation depth — how near the top are you in LLM citations?

Number three, use community as a growth engine. Get involved in the channels where your audience already spends time. Run meetups, local events, bring influencers and experts together. Collect feedback, testimonials, nurture relationships with existing customers.

Number four, optimize for agentic AI and autonomous search. Make sure product availability is clear, pricing is clear, shipping and returns are clear. Strengthen structured data so agents can easily understand your website. Fix crawl issues, avoid JS rendering where possible for critical content. Ensure product data is consistent across your site, schema and feed.

Number five, double down on E-E-A-T with proof. Get mentions and citations from people who use your products. Feature real experts sharing thought leadership and first-person content. Do original research and original data that people want to share organically.

Number six, invest in influence optimization. The battle for attention for B2B brands is moving towards LinkedIn. Invest in influencers to talk about your product on LinkedIn, YouTube, and wherever your audience spends time. Get featured on top podcasts and newsletters in your industry. Standardize your brand narrative across all channels.

Technical SEO still very important. Content marketing still very important. E-E-A-T — all of the fundamentals still very important.
