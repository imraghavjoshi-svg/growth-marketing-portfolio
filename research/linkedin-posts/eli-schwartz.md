**Date:** [Mon, 13 Apr 2026]
**URL:** [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/schwartze_aeo-is-not-seo-20-activity-7449429962782044160-Qv4A?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADmUbgsBpSGfIEqQ4lgjhkT_ZdTbzB0_Zyk]

[AEO is not SEO 2.0.
And everyone treating it like an upgrade has already missed the point.

SEO was always about a funnel that began with search traffic, so technical precision and link volume were key parts of the playbook. You optimized pages, chased rankings, and measured everything in clicks.

LLMs don't have rankings, don't have a page one, and the click stopped being the KPI a while ago. Most people still haven't adjusted their thinking to match that reality.

The part everyone skips over is that you can't manufacture visibility anymore. A scrappy site with the right backlinks could outrank a household name in SEO.

That arbitrage is completely dead now.

LLMs don't respond to domain authority; they respond to whether people actually know who you are, whether your brand gets referenced in the real world.

Citations are not backlinks. You don't acquire your way into an LLM answer by building links or publishing more content.

You get there by being a brand that the world already talked about before you started optimizing anything.

Ranking was something you could game with enough effort and the right tactics. Real trust doesn't work that way. ]

**Date:** [Wed, 25 Mar 2026 ]
**URL:** [https://www.linkedin.com/posts/schwartze_if-your-seo-strategy-needs-a-glossary-it-activity-7442572806334144513-gJDw?utm_source=social_share_send&utm_medium=member_desktop_web&rcm=ACoAADmUbgsBpSGfIEqQ4lgjhkT_ZdTbzB0_Zyk]

[If your SEO strategy needs a glossary, it isn't a strategy.

The entire field of SEO can be summed up in one sentence.

Create great experiences for search users.

And, this is even more true for AEO where the LLMs are smarter.

Except, of course, agencies and in-house teams can't bill for that. So instead you get:

- Logarithmic scoring matrices

- Distributed content weighting frameworks

- Proprietary "OpenClaw modules"

- Tiered authority mapping with decay coefficients

- A 147-slide deck to explain all of the above

And after six months of workshops, audits, and quarterly business reviews, the recommendation is... create great experiences for users.

To be fair, execution is genuinely hard and there are legitimate reasons to build frameworks.

.....But a lot of what gets sold as sophisticated SEO strategy is just performatic. It signals expertise without delivering it.

When it comes to AEO/GEO or whatever it's called this gets even worse because there's an even bigger glossary of words no one knows.

If you're evaluating an SEO/AEO partner or reviewing your internal process, ask one question: does this actually help us build something users find useful and easy to use?

If the answer requires a glossary to understand, that's probably your answer. ]
