## Post 1
**Date:** Tue, 16 Dec 2025
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_people-prefer-ai-written-content-thats-activity-7406774838733807616-w1KD?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop&rcm=ACoAADmUbgsBpSGfIEqQ4lgjhkT_ZdTbzB0_Zyk

People prefer AI-written content.
That's the outcome of a new study from Columbia and MIT by Chakrabarty et al. called "Readers Prefer Outputs of AI Trained on Copyrighted Books over Expert Human Writers."

The Experiment Researchers pitted frontier AI models against MFA-trained expert writers in a head-to-head battle to emulate the styles of Nobel and Booker Prize winning authors.

The results with standard prompting? Predictable. Humans won. The results with Fine-Tuning? Game over.

When AI is properly fine-tuned, it doesn't just mimic human writing - it becomes effectively indistinguishable from it.

The Findings Once the AI was fine-tuned on the author's actual data, the difference between "machine" and "human" evaporated.
- Expert readers were 8X more likely to choose the AI's writing over the human expert's for stylistic accuracy (Odds Ratio = 8.16).
- For general writing quality, the AI won nearly 2-to-1.

This study proves that data is an advantage. By fine-tuning models on your own high-quality content, you can achieve results that aren't just "passable".

We need to stop judging AI based on generic, out-of-the-box prompts. That is like judging a Ferrari by driving it in first gear.
